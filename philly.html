  • Philadelphia Sights

So many people come to see the Liberty Bell, but honestly, we don’t get the hype, I mean its broken bell. But if you’re looking for something historical you can find it over in Old City next to Independence Hall and Ben Franklin’s grave.

There are many Museums in Philadelphia, our favorites are the Rodin Museum and the Barns Foundation.

Queen’s Village is located in center city and has a lot of fun shops, bars and restaurants to try. It also has Philadelphia’s Magic Gardens – an outdoor art space. There are many beautiful outdoor spaces in Philadelphia, such as Rittenhouse Square or Washington Square. These are great places to grab a coffee and enjoy a nice day.

Cheesesteaks are a Philadelphia classic. Many will tell you to eat at Pat’s or Geno’s – BUT DON’T. We would recommend Jim’s on South Street.Cheesesteaks are pretty heavy, I wouldn’t recommend this as a pre wedding meal unless a nap is involved.

Some of our favorite places to eat would be American Sardine Bar (you know, Anthony Bourdain ate here), Tattooed Moms (fun bar food), Burrata (delicious Italian), and if you’re feeling adventurous head on over to West Philly for some Ethiopian at Dalek.
